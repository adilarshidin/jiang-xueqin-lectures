## Overview

Zarathustra enters the bronze age which has capital and all the things that entail it.

This is the time of politheism where you fight for you god. Gods don't care what's good or evil only care about themselves. So you celebrate them and make sacrifices. If you do it good enough and they are pleased they will help you.

Zarathustra places a top god Ahura Mazda, lord of wisdom, represented by fire.

There are 2 forces that divide us constantly: Asha (truth), Druj (lie).

Asha is a system of virtue. By doing good you are closer to Ahura Mazda.

## Kant

Categorial imperative. The moral good we should strive for.

1. Law of universality. Imagine if whatever you do everyone in the world at the same time does it. Act and do and think as though everything you do will be reflected.
2. Free will. Whatever you do can not be coerced. Must be of your own volition, otherwise it's wrong.
3. Humans as the end. Every human life is as valuable as the whole humanity.

System of Asha is what it is.

## Asha

New concepts:

1. Individualism.
2. Free choice.
3. Truth.

These form monotheism.

## Allegory of the cave

We are inside the cave chained and can not move. Looking at the wall, fire behind us projects shadows on the wall. We name the shadows and this is what we call reality. But it is fake. One day chains disappear from one of the people, he climbs out and is suddenly in the light and blinded and in pain. Over time he sees the world and thinks that this is heaven. However to truly achieve it he must go back down and tell others the truth.

This system establishes that truth is not individual. It must be shared with the world or it is not complete.
