# [Game Theory #11: The Law of Escalation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fz-Dan7NRss)

## Theory

Escalation ladder - whoever has a dominant position has an advantage. Who has a gun should win against a knife.

For example 2 people fight. First they run into each other, they argue, it's a conflict. Cursing. Pushing. Punching. Fight starts. Weapons are drawn.

Fight is not contained in itself, there are other players involved, the police, God.

What drives the ladder: emotions, power, reason.

The ladder should be claimed strategically or you will "overreact" and be at fault. Focus, clarity and resolve are needed.

Control is more important than dominance.

## Example

The bully in the school has 4 friends, there are 100 students total. Each bully's friend collects a tax from students to eat in the cafeteria and give the money to him, he splits the money among the gang.

At first it is considered good because the gang provides security and peace in the cafe.

Over time the bully becomes more agressive because he is unchallenged. Starts to charge more. Also he pays to his gang less. Now people are disgruntled.

One day a new guy comes into the school and does not know the game. The gang comes to him and ask for tax, he refuses, they ostracize him and put in the worst spot in the cafe alone, but he doesn't care. Then they decide to start bullying him to force him to pay. However they are ignored.

Slowly other recognize that the game can be overthrown and start to secretly communicate with the guy. The gang itself starts to think that the new guy can be the head or they themselves can be because the bully's authority is under question.

One day they challenge the bully saying what are you going to do, the guy is not afraid. So the bully confronts him directly and the fight starts. The bully wins but he is hurt too. So now everyone saw that he could be taken down and eventually it does happen.

The bully does not have a lot of option, he has to maintain face, credibility. The moment however that he is fought continously he loses it as people see that he is not that strong. This means that he can be manipulated into acting in a way that self-destructs him.
