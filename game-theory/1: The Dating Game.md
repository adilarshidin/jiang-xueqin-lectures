## Game theory

The best way to understand human behaviour, nations, societies.

Game has 3 components: players, rules, incentives.

Knowing 3 components one can understand how the game works and will end.

## Example

5 male and female players ranked according to genes (attractiveness), wealth, status.

|Male|Female|
|----|------|
|5|5|
|4|4|
|3|3|
|2|2|
|1|1|

The best strategy for all the players would be to take their number. Nash equilibrium - you always reach a state where all the players maximize their outcome. However in real life nobody follows the rule.

All 5 females try to procreate with number 5 and maybe 4 but others are out of scope.

And 5 male will go for all the females.

People are rational but you need to understand the game. Here the incentive is not the sex itself or family but status/wealth for women (women are "independent" and childbirth is hard so the man needs to be attractive and/or wealthy). The game changed.

1. Low population society like a village. No competition, poor. Here people do not date, they marry and have children.
2. Growing population society. Wealth, high competition. No dating, need to have as much people as possible to compete with other societies, arranged marriages for that.
3. Overpopulation. Too much wealth (inequality), equilibrium. Dating because to change status you marry up, no other way to do it, decreasing fertility.
