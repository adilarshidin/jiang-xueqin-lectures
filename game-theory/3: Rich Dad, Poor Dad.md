# [Game Theory #3: Rich Dad, Poor Dad](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MX93U4KzA28)

## Theories

1. Walter Mischel marshmallow test. Here success = delayed gratification. People with long term planning succeed.
2. Carol Dwight "Mindset". Growth mindset (improve upon failure) vs fixed mindset (give up upon failure).
3. K. Anders Ericson deliberate practice. Plan: what are the goals, how to achieve them, how to improve the plan. Self-reflection.

Dunning-Kruger experiment. Stupid people lack the capacity to understand their stupidity.

The problem is that teaching this does not help. The reason is that correlation does not equal causation.

Rich -> successful -> growth mindset, self-reflection, deliberate practice. Not the other way around.

## Parenting

|Rich|Poor|
|----|----|
|High vocabulary: speak more|Low vocabulary: no/yes, go away|
|Friendly attitude: you did bad I will explain to you why. The world is safe and he is respected|Authoritarian: never do that again or I'll beat you. The world is scary and you must be afraid of adults|
|Stability: keeping promises|Volatility|

Marshmellow test is not about self-control, it's about trust. If you believe that the experimentator will come back and give another one you will wait. The poor however think that they may not receive the second and are better off eating one.

Resilience. The rich are ready to fail because the world is safe and will help them. The poor can not.

Self-reflection. The poor can only think about pain and stress.

## Society

There is a divide between rich and poor. These 2 worlds are nothing alike.

Poor to survive have to obey authority.

Rich negotiate with others.

So if you are a parent:

1. Poor need to teach children how to obey so they command.
2. Rich need to teach how to negotiate so they respect and talk.

## Breakdown

Playing a game not to improve child's outcome, they play a game to maintain their place.

Police and administration will bully you. If you fight back they imprison you. So you need to obey.

In work you are commanded what to do, follow orders.

In the family it's important to maintain relations with those who can support you therefore having the same values, i.e. the same parenting.

## How some poor succeed

1. Abandon community, high risk.
2. War and revolution.
3. Marriage upwards.

Generally luck.

## The problem

Poor are happy with minimal outcome, low expectations. The rich however always want better and more, high expectations.

Power and hierarchy are zero-sum. Elite overproduction. Revolutions are always between the rich who have everything against rich who want more.
